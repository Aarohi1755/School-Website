campus life

aravali hall

The school has a huge Multi-Purpose Hall – The Aravali Hall with a seating capacity of 300-400 people. The hall is used for Inter-School programmes and school functions. It also doubles up as an indoor games hall.

school library

All classes are provided with at least one library period per week. There are more than 20,000 books on various topics , subjects, the latest fiction, classics and encyclopedias.

audio visual room

Students are also encouraged to make use of this room for presentations by them for different projects. The air-conditioned AV Room facilitates workshop for students and teachers at regular intervals.

RENAISSANCE 2021-Vivekanand Public School, Anand Vihar

Vivekanand Public School, Anand Vihar, New Delhi organised an online Interschool fest Renaissance 2021 on 20th Nov, 2021. Our student Aarohi Nanda brought laurels to the school by securing third position in the event 'Ted Talk'. The theme of the event was 'Do good feel good .... Uprise humanity'. In the event the participant had to speak about one incident that made him/her feel good. Aarohi Nanda, of class V B, spoke very confidently about an act of kindness performed by her while keeping the viewers engaged with her energetic performance. The participants were judged on the basis of content, thought clarity, originality and confidence wherein Aarohi proved her mettle.


what's new

  • CELEBRATING NATIONAL YOUTH DAY- JUNIOR SCHOOL


    Swami Vivekananda, the young and vibrant saint from India left everyone speechless at the World Parliament of Religion in Chicago in 1893, when he addressed the gathering as, “My brothers and sisters of America”. The profound impact he created in that moment remains unforgettable in the history of World Peace and Unity. The Birth Anniversary of Swami Vivekananda is commemorated across the country as the National Youth Day, celebrating the spirit of youthfulness and the inspiring way of life led by this great intellectual, social and spiritual icon. TAFS,Junior Wing too observed the National Youth Day on 12 Jan 22, with the purpose of making children aware of the principles and ideals of Swami Vivekananda and their relevance in present times.The students were introduced to the life lessons of the patriot saint through a presentation. A special assembly was conducted and the motivating stories of Swami Vivekananda’s life were shared. Class 2 was shown a video on the life of Swami Vivekananda which was followed by a fun quiz based on it. Class 3 too learnt about Swami Vivekananda through a video and specific incidents of his life were shared in the class. A short quiz was conducted for Classes 4 and 5 after a presentation on the life of Swami Vivekananda and the students also submitted drawings,paintings and posters based on his life. The children also dressed in hues of saffron as Swami Vivekananda were a sight to behold. Swami Vivekananda’s teachings continue to reverberate in our Indian ethos and the onus is upon us to draw valuable learnings from them. “Arise, Awake and Stop Not till the Goal is reached.”

  • REPORT ON WEBINAR : INTERACTION WITH SUCCESSFUL PROFESSIONALS


    A webinar was organised on ‘Interaction with Successful Professionals’ by EDUtv on Wednesday, 12 th Jan at 3 pm on zoom platform. The session was attended by students of grade XIIth . Mr Kunwar Shekhar Vijendra, Chancellor, Shobhit University was the key speaker of the event. In the event he spoke about skills that the youth must develop. There are two types of skills i.e. life skills and technical Skills. Under Life skills, he spoke about curiosity, courage, compassion, collaboration and communication. And in technical skills he spoke about computing skills and economics. With this he also spoke about the development of leadership skills. To inspire students he shared anecdotes from his personal life. Overall, it was a very motivating session in which the speaker emphasised upon skill development.
